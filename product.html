Product Introduction

The Science Behind Long-Term Corrosion Protection

PS material is an innovative industrial coating formed by the cross-linked reaction of modified resin and modified silicate. This unique chemistry combines two performance worlds: the chemical resistance of inorganic silicate and the mechanical resilience of organic isocyanate — producing a dense, tenacious protective coating layer that conventional single-chemistry coatings cannot replicate.

The result is a coating that delivers simultaneous, long-term resistance to strong acids, strong alkalis, high salinity, crude oils, organic solvents, oxidants, and most other industrial chemicals — while remaining non-toxic, solvent-free, and safe to apply and transport.

PS material is applied by brushing, rolling, spraying, or scratching on carbon steel, stainless steel, alloys, concrete, and other substrates — curing at room temperature without special equipment. This combination of performance breadth and application ease makes it uniquely suited to the complex corrosion challenges of the petroleum, petrochemical, marine, power, and industrial sectors.

PS Material Key Advantages

Every advantage below is test-validated and confirmed on actual industrial projects across petroleum, marine, and chemical processing environments.

Long-Term Corrosion Resistance

12-month laboratory-confirmed resistance against strong acids, strong alkalis, high salinity, crude oil, gasoline, kerosene, and organic solvents. No blistering, creping, cracking, exfoliation, or rusting observed after full test duration at standard temperature conditions.

Environmentally Friendly

VOC content: Not Detected (GBT 23986-2009). Solvent-free formulation with no heavy metals, no carcinogens, and no hazardous chemical classification. Safe to transport by air, sea, road, and rail as non-dangerous goods under IATA DGR and IMDG Code.

Flame Retardant — UL 94 V0

Achieves UL 94 V0 classification and B1 fire resistance rating (GB 8624-2012). Resin flash point of 230°C. Vertical burn test certified as best flame resistant material — critical for petroleum, chemical, and power generation environments.

High Adhesion & Mechanical Strength

Exceptional adhesion to carbon steel, stainless steel, alloys, and concrete substrates without primer requirement. High impact resistance, excellent toughness, outstanding wear resistance, and good temperature variation resistance — withstanding thermal shocks from large temperature fluctuations.

Easy Application

Applied by brushing, rolling, scratching, or spraying directly onto prepared substrate surfaces. Cures at room temperature without specialized equipment or elevated temperature curing chambers. Primerless formulation reduces application steps and construction cost.

Recognised by Industry Standards

Listed as a key supporting technology for environmental protection and cleaner production in the petroleum and chemical industry by CPCIF/CCEPA (2018). Performance approved on actual industrial projects including petroleum, marine, and chemical processing applications.

Project Case Studies

PS material performance has been validated on real industrial projects in demanding operating environments. The following examples demonstrate its capability under extreme corrosive and thermal conditions.

Natural Gas / Petroleum
Operational

Anti-Corrosion Protection of Natural Gas Drilling Mud Circulation Tank

PetroChina Southwest Company
Challenge: Drilling mud circulation tanks in natural gas field operations operate at designed temperatures of 120°C, contain H₂S, and generate severe corrosive wear on coating layers. Traditional inorganic coatings experienced delamination after repeated thermal cycling due to high mud temperatures and large thermal shock differentials — requiring costly factory repair and project downtime.
Solution: PS material was applied for internal anti-corrosion protection of the mud circulation tanks. The polymer silicate chemistry withstands long-term thermal shocks from large temperature variations, with no VOC emission during enclosed-space tank application — providing a safer construction environment than traditional anti-corrosion methods.

Chemical / TDI Production
3 Years Operational

Caustic Washing Tower — TDI Exhaust Gas Treatment Facility

Chemical Processing Facility
Challenge: TDI production generates exhaust gases including HCl, H₂S, and phosgene. The caustic washing towers treating these gases — operating at 60–90°C with NaOH (5–20%) and NaCl (<15%) on carbon steel substrate — suffered from GFRP lining brittleness, peeling, and premature failure, leading to tank erosion and forced production shutdowns.
Solution: PS polymer silicate material replaced the original GFRP lining, providing long-term simultaneous resistance to strong acids, strong alkalis, and high salinity at the defined operating conditions. Project was completed in 2022.
